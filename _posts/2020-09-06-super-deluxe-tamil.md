To say *Super Deluxe* stands out amidst the mostly homogeneous backdrop of Indian films is an understatement. The film juggles four different genre-blending stories while also providing stark social commentary on a plethora of topics. The opening scenes alone would be enough to enrage or alienate large subsections of a standard Indian audience. And yet, the film maintains an 8.4 of 10 rating with more than 8,000 reviews on IMDB, a respectable feat given it's philosophical tendencies and preachy messages. It's clear that people really connect with this film, despite, or maybe because of its boldness.

In retrospect, none of this is really surprising. Thiagarajan Kumarraraja is no stranger to controversial films. His first attempt at directing a feature film, Aaranya Kaandam, ran into many issues with the Chennai censor board in 2011. The film then went on to win national and regional awards and became a cult hit. 8 years later, his second directorial feature, *Super Deluxe*, gave Kummarraraja something new along with the accolades: commercial success. 

I knew none of this before watching the film. I had only heard of *Super Deluxe* in passing as a movie worth checking out. Thus, last week when I sat down to watch the movie virtually with a Tamil-speaking friend, I was blissfully unaware of the shocking, humorous and outright absurd three-hour adventure that lay ahead.

# Humans Are Divisive And Ignorant
If you want to only focus on one of the many topics that *Super Deluxe* touches on, it would be this one. Kummarraraja's film depicts characters in various situations facing conflicts because of the human tendency to separate "us" from "them". The unfamiliar, whether that's through caste, religion or something simpler like gender identity, is often seen as dangerous or not worthy of inclusion. 

## Caste
Anyone familiar with Indian society doesn't need an introduction to the divisiveness of the caste system. Various films have portrayed this issues that "lower caste" members of society face on a daily basis. *Super Deluxe* doesn't actually spend a lot of time on this concept. Instead, film addresses it briefly when Mugil and his wife, Vaembu, argue about their arranged marriage. In a moment of defensiveness, Mugil exclaims that countries are not really much different from castes. Why is it okay to feel patriotic for one's country, but not be supportive of one's caste? 

While it's a brief moment, this rebuttal drives home a simple concept about division. Society will normalize some forms of division while still fighting against others. It just depends on the whims of what society will agree is acceptable and what isn't.

## Gender Identity
One of the main characters of Super Deluxe, Shilpa, is transgender. Her character is introduced almost as a joke: what's the worst thing that a woman can fear from a husband returning home after a long time away? Side characters gossip about the husband coming home with a second family. No one dreams the husband would return transformed, finally embracing her true gender identity as woman. 

Yet, Shilpa is unapologetic upon her arrival after a long absence. Instead, much of the actual dialogue about her female identity is revealed through conversations with her son. This method of dialogue is important: it simplifies the concept of gender identity in a way that uninitiated adults can understand without breaking the plot of the film. If anything, the conversations between parent and son are endearing. Their relationship might even be the strongest one in the entire film.

Some of the struggles that transgender people face in real life are clearly depicted throughout the film. Invalidated, embarrassed and outright ignored - these are all emotions that we see Shilpa go through during her emotion-driven homecoming. The difference in interactions is also something that her son doesn't accept. Seeing the parent son interactions play out in the film drive another major point: humans aren't divisive by nature. It's a behavior that we learn, cultivated and enforced by other members in society and their actions.

## Women and Sex
Sex is a recurring theme in Super Deluxe. The young boys (Gaaji, Soori, Mohan, Vasanth, and Thuyavan) skip school to watch a porn film at home. Shilpa returns home with a gender identity normally attributed to the opposite sex. Vaembu commits adultery. Leela is an actress who has appeared in porn. All four story lines begin with this theme in a pretty bold exposition for an Indian cinema.

Both Leela and Vaembu have sex that is not approved of by society and face outsized consequences because of it. Leela's story focuses more on her family and how they view her previous actions of acting in a porn film. Embarrassed and degraded for her actions by her own son, Leela persists as a strong female figure and mother throughout the film. While most people would consider Vaembu's extramarital sex as morally wrong, the film focuses more on how that one action is used as an excuse by various men to add increasingly wild labels to her character. This culminates in an attempt at rape, where her character is assumed to be more okay with being forced to have sex for favors. Once again, the message here is clear. Women who have sex that isn't approved by society are judged, labeled, degraded and even denied essential human rights.

## We Are All One
By combining all of these stories and more in a single theme, *Super Deluxe* asserts that humans are divisive and ignorant of a simple fact: we are all one. We share more in common than not; we share the same building blocks of life, the same emotions that move us and the same desire to be happy. Nevertheless, humanity continues to fabricate separations into a society that then becomes toxic for those who face the short straw.

Fear of Losing Reputation is Dangerous
A smaller theme throughout *Super Deluxe* is the importance that people tend to place on their reputations. All of the conflicts in the film revolve around the characters' fear of losing face, whether that's amidst friends, family or the public. Instead of facing the consequences of their actions, the characters are willing to go through with increasingly wild and dangerous plans to save something that is both hard to quantify and ultimately meaningless.

## Faith is a Personal Journey
Faith is another sub theme explored by the film. Leela's son's father, Arputham, goes through a crisis of faith and the film toys with this concept throughout its progression. While I felt some of these scenes were the weakest links in the movie, this theme is also interesting because it is open-ended. Like Arputham, many people struggle with faith throughout their lives and Kummarraraja refuses to make a statement here. It seems his goal was to get the audience to question their own faith and arrive at their own conclusions on what they believe.

# Conclusion

It's really fun to watch the four stories of *Super Deluxe* unfold, but the film is especially memorable for it's thought provoking themes and attempts to get the audience to engage in its philosophy. While it is kind of long, I highly recommend giving it a watch as long as you're willing to approach the concepts with an open mind and not easily be offended by it's audacity. If you aren't, I think it's still worth a watch for the stories. And who knows, you might even leave the film feeling a little out of this world.
